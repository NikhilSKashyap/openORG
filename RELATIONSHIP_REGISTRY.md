# Relationship Registry 0.1

Relationships are portable `LineageAssertion` records. Providers may index or
visualize them differently, but their meaning and lifecycle remain stable.

| Relationship   | From                    | To                    | Typical basis        |
| -------------- | ----------------------- | --------------------- | -------------------- |
| `derived_from` | claim, signal, artifact | source                | observed             |
| `supports`     | signal, evidence        | claim, decision       | asserted             |
| `contradicts`  | signal, evidence        | claim, decision       | asserted             |
| `constrains`   | constraint              | decision, work        | asserted             |
| `motivates`    | signal, claim           | decision              | asserted or inferred |
| `authorizes`   | decision, approval      | work                  | observed             |
| `commits_to`   | commitment              | outcome, artifact     | asserted             |
| `implements`   | work, artifact          | decision              | observed             |
| `produces`     | work                    | artifact              | observed             |
| `verifies`     | verification            | work, artifact        | observed             |
| `measures`     | outcome                 | decision, artifact    | observed             |
| `corrects`     | correction              | claim, decision, work | asserted             |
| `supersedes`   | newer record            | older record          | observed             |

Every assertion records `basis`, `state`, `authority`, `evidenceRefs`, and a
timestamp. A semantic relationship generated by an agent begins as `proposed`.
A deterministic connector may confirm mechanical relationships under a named
policy. Rejections and supersessions remain queryable.
