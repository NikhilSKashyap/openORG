# Trademark policy

The Apache License 2.0 gives broad permission to use, modify, and distribute
the openORG code. It does not grant permission to use the project's names or
logos as your own brand.

This policy covers the names openORG, Organizational Record Protocol, ORP,
Organizational Learning Protocol, OLP, and the project's logos and visual
identity (the "openORG marks"). These marks may be unregistered.

## Uses that do not need permission

You may:

- refer truthfully to openORG, ORP, or OLP;
- say that a product implements, supports, or is compatible with a protocol,
  provided that the statement is accurate;
- link to the project or quote its documentation;
- redistribute an unmodified release with its original name and notices; and
- run a community group or event whose name makes clear that it is independent
  and unofficial.

Use only as much of a mark as needed to identify the project. Do not imply
sponsorship, certification, or endorsement.

## Uses that need permission

Please ask the project maintainers before:

- using an openORG mark in a company, product, service, or domain name;
- using a project logo as your own logo or app icon;
- calling a modified distribution an official openORG release; or
- claiming that the project has certified or endorsed your implementation.

## Forks and modified distributions

The license permits forks. A fork that is presented as a separate product must
use a different product name and visual identity. It may say, truthfully, that
it is based on openORG and must preserve the license and attribution notices.

## Compatibility claims

"ORP-compatible" and "OLP-compatible" should describe implementations that
pass the applicable published conformance tests. A compatibility claim is not
an endorsement by the openORG project.

Questions and permission requests may be opened with the project maintainers
through the repository. This policy may be clarified over time, but it will not
take away rights already granted to the code under Apache-2.0.
